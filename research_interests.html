Areas of interest: Semantics, pragmatics, psycholinguistics

Topics of interest: Argument structure, mental representation, language of thought, discourse inference, quantifiers

Problem 1: How is meaning represented when we are thinking -- with what are we reasoning? I would like to investigate into the primitives of mental logic or the language of thought. What about the meaning of questions? This is what I aim to look into with my project in the mental representation of wh-words supervised by Prof. Matt Husband.

Problem 2: How do we express things when we don't say something, or choose not to say something? This is what I'm doing in my Master's thesis, where I aim to explain how temporal information is computed in Mandarin Chinese from discourse, where time can be left unspecified. I am also doing a project with Prof. Brendan Gillon, looking into the interpretations of optional complements in Mandarin Chinese ditransitive verbs when they are omitted.
